ABOUT THE ROCHESTER, NH
HISTORIC PRESERVATION AWARENESS PROJECT

The Rochester, NH Historic Preservation Awareness Project is a research project that is being conducted by a group of UNH students to identify why there is a lack of historic preservation awareness in the City of Rochester. In order to find that out they will be conducting research to answer the following research questions.

  1. How is the lack of awareness of historic preservation hindering historic preservation efforts in Rochester?
  2. What is the importance of historic preservation awareness?
  3. What can be done in Rochester to create a greater understanding of Historic Preservation?

After answering those questions they will come up with several different proposals that could be used to foster a greater essence of historic preservation awareness in the city of Rochester.

Built at the same time as the City’s Carnegie-funded public library, the Annex was part of the first wave of new civic structures in the City of Rochester. There where three reseach methods used in the research, a focus group, one key person interview, and case study research.

Results

Throughout the research there were three common trends that we identified between all of the research methods.

  1. A strong Historic District Commission is needed to advocate for historic preservation and spread historic preservation awareness in Rochester. That is because the citizens “see” the HDC and historic preservation as the same, so if the HDC is seen in a bad light, so will historic preservation.
  2. Creative ways are needed to spread historic preservation awareness and to get more citizens involved in historic preservation. That is because the old ways of getting people involved are no longer working.
  3. That historic preservation needs to be a collaborative effort between the government and private industry. That is because there needs to be a common ground between historic preservation and development.
