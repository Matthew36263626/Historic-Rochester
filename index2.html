Brief History of Rochester

Rochester was originally called Norway Plains in 1722. The area was not settled until 1729. The main occupation of the settlers was Lumbering. “In addition to lumbering, economic vitality came to Rochester, due in part to being blessed with three rivers, the Cocheco, Salmon Falls and Isinglass. These rivers provided fertile land for farming and water power for industrial growth.”
